We Make Every Dollar Count

Your contribution makes a direct impact on the community. Choose a donation amount and help fund meaningful programs that uplift, educate, and support those in need.

Where Your Donations Go

Each contribution helps fund these key initiatives:

Education

Scholarship Fund

Four $1,000 scholarships awarded annually to Woodland and Streator students.

Youth Sports

Woodland Basketball HUDL System

Over $5,000 contributed to support the team’s HUDL video system since 2021.

Community

Local Club Donation

$1,000 donated annually to clubs like Key Club, FFA, and others supporting youth leadership.

Literacy

Books for Bikes

Rewarding young readers at Kimes, Woodland, and Centennial Elementary Schools with bicycles.

Warmth

Coat Drive

Providing warm winter coats to children who need them most.

Youth Engagement

Kids Fun Nights

Free social events that bring joy, activity, and community to local youth.

Compassion

Shop With a Cop

Helping local children enjoy a brighter holiday season by partnering with law enforcement to provide gifts, clothing, and essentials during a special shopping experience.

Brotherhood

Widows & Shut‑in Brothers

Support for those facing isolation, hardship, or financial difficulty.

Academics

Streator Scholastic Bowl Team

Encouraging academic excellence and teamwork through competition support.

Holiday Tradition

Breakfast with Santa

Free breakfast, photos, and presents for local children each holiday season.

Facilities

Kids Rec Room Renovations

Enhancing recreational spaces to create a safe, fun environment for youth.

Inclusion

Helping Kids with Disabilities

Supporting children with physical and developmental disabilities through inclusive programs, adaptive resources, and community‑centered activities that ensure every child feels valued, supported, and empowered.

Why Donate?

Your generosity fuels programs that make a tangible difference in our community. Whether it’s education, support, or enrichment, your gift helps strengthen Streator’s future.
