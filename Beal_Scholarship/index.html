Masonic Scholarships

Each year, Illinois Freemasonry awards scholarships to Masons, their families, Masonic youth, and the public to support higher education and the pursuit of knowledge.

Duane R. Beal Annual Scholarship

Supporting the Next Generation Since 2020

Download Application

The Duane R. Beal Annual Scholarship was established in 2020 to honor Brother Duane R. Beal Jr., a dedicated Mason whose life embodied service, mentorship, and a deep commitment to education.

Each year, Streator Lodge No. 607 awards this scholarship to a few deserving local students who demonstrate academic promise, strong character, and a commitment to bettering their community.

This scholarship reflects our lodge’s belief that investing in young people strengthens not only their future, but the future of our entire community.

Purpose of the Scholarship

The scholarship was created to:

  • Recognize outstanding students in the Streator area
  • Encourage academic achievement and personal growth
  • Support young adults who demonstrate leadership, service, and integrity
  • Honor the memory and values of Brother Duane R. Beal

Since its founding, the scholarship has helped local students pursue degrees in a wide range of fields, from engineering and education to healthcare and the trades.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Graduating high school senior from Streator or Woodland High Schools
  • Planning to attend a college, university, or accredited trade program
  • Demonstrated academic effort and personal responsibility
  • Involvement in school, community, or volunteer activities
  • Submission of all required application materials by the deadline

Application Materials

  • Completed scholarship application form
  • Short personal essay (300–500 words)
  • One letter of recommendation
  • High school transcript
  • Optional: documentation of community service or extracurricular involvement

Award Information

The scholarship amount is determined annually based on available funds and community support. Recipients are recognized at a lodge meeting or community event, where they and their families are invited to attend.

How to Apply

Applications typically open in early spring each year. Students may download the application form from this page or request one through their school counselor.

Completed applications can be submitted:

  • By their school counselor (preferred)
  • By email to Scholarship Committee Chairman
  • In person at Streator Lodge No. 607
  • By mail (postmarked by the deadline)

Full submission instructions are included on the application form.
